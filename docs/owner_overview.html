Why it exists

The gap isn't the survey. It's everything between the survey and the deliverable.

Ekahau tells you how the network performs. Your clients, though, don't get handed a heatmap — they get a report: a summary, the scope, the pass/fail criteria, findings, and as-built photo documentation for every access point. Assembling that by hand, job after job, is where the hours go.

The most tedious part is stitching two field photos to the right AP, by name, dozens of times per site. It's tedious enough that it's the first thing to get rushed or skipped.

This tool makes that step free — and loud the moment a photo's AP name doesn't match the survey, so nothing lands in a client's report wrong and quiet.

How it works

One path, six steps — from the phone in the field to the file the client opens.

1
Capture

Field photos

The tech shoots Close + Far of each AP on a phone, tags the exact AP name, uploads. Nothing else runs on the device.

2
Parse

Read the survey

Each Ekahau .esx is read into a clean AP list — names, models, band, channel, power. Multi-floor jobs combine.

3
Merge

Join on AP name

Photos meet survey APs on exact name. A match pairs up. A mismatch is flagged — never guessed, never silently merged.

4
Resolve

Clear the flags

The drafter settles each flag. One shared reason — "Ceiling access denied" — can apply to many APs at once.

5
Generate

Branded report

A Word document is filled in your branding: summary, scope, criteria, findings, per-AP inventory with photos, appendices.

6
Approve

Sign off & lock

An owner reviews and signs off. The job locks. What comes out is the file your client receives.

The rule

When a photo's AP name doesn't match the survey — or two survey files disagree about the same AP — the tool stops and flags it for a person to decide. It will never quietly pair the wrong photo to an AP. That single rule is what keeps every report trustworthy.

Who does what

Roles are hats, not people.

In the field

Tech

Captures Close + Far and the exact AP name, then uploads. No merging or processing on the phone.

At the desk

Drafter / Editor

Pushes the merge, clears flags, writes the summary and recommendations, generates the document, owns the deliverable.

Sign-off

Approver

Reviews and locks the job — usually Josh or Jeff. Once approved, the report is final.

On a solo job, one person wears all three hats. On a team job, it's a clean handoff — capture → draft → approve — with the tool tracking where each job is.
